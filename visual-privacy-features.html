The Physics of Virtual Frosted Glass

Like real frosted glass, visibility in MeetingGlass requires mutual presence:

  • You at the glass? (Camera ON) ‚Üí You see others ‚Üí Others see you
  • You step away? (Camera OFF) ‚Üí You see nothing ‚Üí You're invisible

This isn't an option ‚Äî it's the fundamental design of our virtual glass technology.

1. Visibility is Mutual

Your camera ON = See others

Like real glass: No one-way viewing. No surveillance. Just comfortable, reciprocal presence.

2. Frosted by Default

Even when visible, you appear behind frosted glass. Others see your presence but not the details of what you are doing. Enough to show you're there, not enough to distract or expose.

3. Click to Unfrost

Click to gradually unfrost a user. Visual feedback shows the unfrosting process. Automatically re-frosts after interaction to maintain ongoing privacy.

4. Confirm Unfrost

When someone requests to see you clearly, you decide if you will be unfrosted or not. Maintain control over your visibility at all times.

5. Different Privacy Levels

Choose a comfortable privacy level for meeting participants based on context: work meetings, study sessions, or social gatherings. Customize team privacy settings.

6. Convenient Video Modes

Select video modes to be applied to every meeting participant. Ensures consistent experience across the entire meeting.

7. Equal sizes of everyone's live videos

Size represents the "distance" between users. You set up the size of live video feeds for all users, creating an equitable visual experience for everyone.

This page details the virtual frosted glass technology features of the MeetingGlass video meeting application. MeetingGlass recreates the natural privacy of physical frosted glass in digital video meetings through several key features: 1) Mandatory mutual visibility (you only see others when your camera is on), 2) Frosted appearance by default (users appear as comfortable blurs), 3) Click-to-unfrost functionality with confirmation, 4) Adjustable privacy levels for different meeting contexts, 5) Consistent video modes applied to all participants, 6) Equal video sizes for equitable visual experience, and 7) User-controlled unfrost confirmation. This technology is designed for remote work teams, study groups, and social meetings where comfortable presence without surveillance is desired. The virtual frosted glass fundamentally changes video meetings by providing reciprocal privacy, reducing meeting fatigue, and enabling natural collaboration.
